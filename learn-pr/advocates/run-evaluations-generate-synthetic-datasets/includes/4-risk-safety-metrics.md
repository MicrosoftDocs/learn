Risk and safety metrics help you identify harmful, unsafe, or otherwise undesirable output before you ship a generative AI application. When you run these evaluators across a dataset, Foundry aggregates row-level results into a *defect rate* (the share of rows that exceed the configured severity threshold for severity-based evaluators, or that return `True` for boolean evaluators) so that you can see how often the application produces unwanted content. These metrics help you:

- **Measure harm.** Identify and quantify harmful content generated by AI systems so that they don't spread misinformation, hate speech, or other malicious content.
- **Inform mitigation strategy.** Surface actionable insights that you can use to plan mitigations for identified risks and improve the overall safety of the application.
- **Build trust.** Demonstrate a commitment to safety and responsibility in your AI deployments.

## Scenario

Contoso Tales is developing an app that generates creative, age-appropriate campfire stories for children, tailored to their reading levels. The app builds on the story plot with input from the reader to craft unique, imaginative stories.

## Instructions

In this exercise, you assess an AI-generated excerpt for harm using risk and safety metrics. Open the `evaluate-risk-safety.ipynb` file to get started.

## Metrics

The following table summarizes the safety evaluators relevant to this workshop. Severity-based evaluators return a score on a 0-7 scale that maps to **Very Low (0-1)**, **Low (2-3)**, **Medium (4-5)**, or **High (6-7)**. The boolean evaluators return `True` or `False`.

> [!TIP]
> Safety evaluators use the hosted Foundry Evaluation service and require a Foundry project in a supported region. They don't use the Azure OpenAI deployment that the quality evaluators rely on, so you don't need to pass `model_config` to them—pass `azure_ai_project` and `credential` instead.

| Evaluator | What it measures | Required inputs | Per-row output |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Hate and Unfairness | Language that reflects hate toward, or unfair representation of, individuals or social groups. | `query`, `response` | Severity score 0-7 plus severity band |
| Sexual | Sexual content, sexual abuse, or otherwise explicit material. | `query`, `response` | Severity score 0-7 plus severity band |
| Violence | Violent, threatening, or weapon-related content. | `query`, `response` | Severity score 0-7 plus severity band |
| Self-Harm | Content related to injuring one's body or taking one's own life. | `query`, `response` | Severity score 0-7 plus severity band |
| Indirect Attack (XPIA) | Detects indirect prompt-injection (XPIA) signals in the response, such as evidence that the model followed manipulated content, attempted intrusion, or unauthorized information gathering planted in retrieved or external content. | `query`, `response` | `True` (attack detected) or `False` |
| Protected Material | Whether the response contains protected text such as song lyrics, recipes, or news articles drawn from copyrighted sources. | `query`, `response` | `True` (protected material detected) or `False` |

### Composite safety evaluator

If you want to run the four severity-based harm evaluators together, the SDK provides a composite:

| Evaluator | What it bundles |
|---------|---------|
| `ContentSafetyEvaluator` | Runs `ViolenceEvaluator`, `SexualEvaluator`, `SelfHarmEvaluator`, and `HateUnfairnessEvaluator` together and returns their combined results for a single query/response pair. Like the underlying evaluators, it uses the hosted Foundry Evaluation service via `azure_ai_project` and `credential`. |

For the full reference and current region requirements, see [Risk and safety evaluators](/azure/foundry/concepts/evaluation-evaluators/risk-safety-evaluators) and [Rate limits, region support, and enterprise features for evaluation](/azure/foundry/concepts/evaluation-regions-limits-virtual-network).
