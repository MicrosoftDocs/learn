## Unit overview

AI adoption succeeds when users understand _how_ to work with AI systems, _how_ to craft effective prompts, and _how_ to use AI responsibly within organizational guardrails. This unit equips solution architects to design user-centric training programs that build AI literacy, prompt fluency, and responsible usage habits.

This unit draws on Microsoft Learn guidance for:

- Empowering users with accessible AI tools

- Driving transformation with Azure AI platforms

- Embedding AI governance into training and adoption

## Learning objectives

By the end of this unit, learners will be able to:

- Identify user personas and their AI training needs

- Design prompt-training programs aligned with business workflows

- Teach users how to interact responsibly with AI systems

- Integrate accessibility and inclusive design into AI training

- Build training assets that accelerate adoption and reduce friction

## Why user and prompt training is essential for AI adoption

AI tools amplify human capability—but only when users understand:

- How to ask effective questions

- How to structure prompts

- How to validate AI output

- How to use AI responsibly

- How AI fits into their workflow

Microsoft Learn emphasizes that AI adoption requires skilling, accessibility, and governance, not just technology deployment.

### Training goals

- Build user confidence

- Improve prompt quality

- Reduce errors and misuse

- Increase productivity and adoption

- Ensure responsible and ethical use

## Identify user personas and training needs

Different users require different training approaches. Based on Microsoft Learn's guidance on AI empowerment and transformation, common personas include:

### Everyday business users

Training focus:

- Basic prompting

- Using AI for summaries, drafting, and analysis

- Understanding limitations and responsible use

### Power users / AI champions

Training focus:

- Advanced prompting

- Multi-step workflows

- Creating reusable prompt templates

- Coaching peers

### Managers & decision makers

Training focus:

- Reviewing AI-generated insights

- Understanding governance boundaries

- Monitoring usage and outcomes

### Accessibility-focused users

Training focus:

- Using AI tools to support accessibility needs

- Voice-based prompting

- Inclusive design features

This aligns with Microsoft's emphasis on AI accessibility and inclusive empowerment.

## Core prompt training curriculum

Your training program should teach users:

### How AI interprets prompts

- AI does not infer intent

- Clarity and specificity matter

- Context improves accuracy

- Constraints reduce incorrect information

### Prompt patterns users should learn

- Instruction + Context + Output

- Role prompting

- Few-shot examples

- Multi-step prompts

- Correction prompts

### Workflow-integrated prompting

From Azure transformation guidance:

- Prompts should align with business processes

- Users must understand where AI fits into the workflow

- Training should include real business scenarios

### Responsible prompting

From AI governance guidance:

- Avoid sensitive data in prompts

- Follow organizational guardrails

- Validate AI output before use

- Understand bias and limitations

## Designing the user training program

A complete training program includes:

### Awareness training

- What the AI solution does

- What it does _not_ do

- Responsible use expectations

- Accessibility features

### Prompt literacy training

- Core prompting skills

- Hands-on practice

- Prompt anti-patterns

### Workflow-integrated training

- How AI supports the business process

- How to use AI tools in context

- When to escalate to a human

### Reinforcement & coaching

- Office hours

- AI champions

- Prompt library access

- In-app guidance

## Building training assets

Your training program should include:

### Prompt playbooks

- Task-specific examples

- Do/Don't lists

- Troubleshooting guidance

### Prompt library

- Standardized templates

- Domain-specific prompts

- Versioning and governance

### Scenario-based exercises

Examples:

- Drafting customer communications

- Summarizing meeting notes

- Analyzing data trends

- Creating lesson plans (from educator AI guidance)

### Accessibility-friendly materials

- Voice-based prompting examples

- Screen-reader-friendly instructions

- Inclusive design considerations

### In-app guidance

- Tooltips

- Suggested prompts

- Embedded examples
