In this module, you explored the governance landscape in Microsoft Fabric. You saw which capabilities come with every Fabric license — organizing your data estate with tenants, domains, and workspaces; controlling access through workspace roles and data-level security; helping users find trustworthy data through the OneLake catalog, endorsement, and lineage; and monitoring activity with the Monitoring Hub and Capacity Metrics App.

You then learned which governance scenarios require a Microsoft Purview license: automatically classifying sensitive data with sensitivity labels, preventing unauthorized sharing with DLP policies, building a searchable enterprise data catalog, detecting insider threats with IRM, governing AI interactions in Fabric Copilot, and maintaining a compliance audit trail.

Finally, you explored the OneLake catalog Govern tab — the place in Fabric where Purview-powered insights surface: sensitivity label coverage, DLP policy status, endorsement and freshness data, and recommended actions to improve your governance posture.
