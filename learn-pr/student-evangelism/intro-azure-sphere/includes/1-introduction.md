Azure Sphere is a secured, high-level application platform for internet-connected devices built on secured, connected, crossover microcontroller units (MCUs). A microcontroller is a single-chip computer that combines a processor, storage, memory, and device I/O capabilities for embedded scenarios.

Many IoT solutions historically secured individual components, such as hardware, device software, or network communication, rather than treating the full device-to-cloud system as a single security boundary. That gap is especially important for small, constrained, microcontroller-powered devices and for older equipment that was deployed before secure-by-default connectivity was common. Azure Sphere was designed to help create an end-to-end trusted environment for connected devices and for brownfield equipment that connects through an Azure Sphere device, such as a [guardian module](/azure-sphere/hardware/guardian-modules?view=azure-sphere-integrated&preserve-view=true).

Suppose you're part of the engineering team responsible for solar-panel monitoring. Your team manages the controllers, inverters, and gateways that collect operational data from solar arrays. Some sites use newer connected controllers, while other remote sites use legacy equipment that needs a gateway or guardian module to add secure connectivity. As part of a green initiative, your company plans to expand its solar estate. You need to monitor the health of the solar arrays, deploy application updates to connected monitoring controllers or guardian modules, and send product telemetry to cloud services for analysis. These tasks must be done remotely and securely.
