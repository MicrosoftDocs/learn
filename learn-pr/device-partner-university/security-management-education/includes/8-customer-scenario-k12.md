## Customer scenario: NYC Public Schools (K–12)

[NYC Public Schools](https://www.microsoft.com/customers/story/1777751286305730349-new-york-city-public-schools-microsoft-intune-higher-education-en-united-states?msockid=2d94348f3bec6ddc210a22003fec6b91) serves more than 750,000 students across 1,800 schools. When the district needed to support remote learning at scale, it faced the same kinds of challenges described above: rapid deployment of devices, equitable access to digital tools, and a consistent experience for students regardless of location or connectivity.

The district operated a mixed device fleet, including Windows devices, iPads, and Chromebooks. Many students didn't have reliable home internet access, requiring LTE-enabled devices. The need for centralized management was acute; without a unified system for monitoring devices, enforcing updates, and deploying applications, learning continuity could have been severely disrupted.

> [!NOTE]
>
> **What is LTE?**
>
> LTE (Long-Term Evolution) is a type of cellular network that provides high-speed internet access over 4G or 5G connections. In the context of education, LTE-enabled devices allow students to connect to cloud resources, learning apps, and online lessons even when they don’t have reliable home Wi-Fi. This ensures that all students can participate in remote or hybrid learning, helping schools maintain equitable access to digital tools and uninterrupted instruction.

To address this, NYC Public Schools implemented Microsoft Intune for Education to manage its device fleet. Microsoft Intune for Education allowed administrators to configure devices remotely, apply updates automatically, and enforce security policies across all schools. By adopting Windows Autopilot, the district was able to provision devices quickly and consistently, ensuring that students received ready-to-learn devices without needing to physically visit IT offices. Windows Autopilot’s zero-touch deployment accelerated device roll out and reduced the burden on IT teams.

With this system in place, NYC Public Schools achieved centralized management of its entire fleet, enabling administrators to deploy, patch, and update applications at scale. LTE-enabled devices allowed students to participate in remote learning even without home Wi-Fi. Microsoft Intune for Education provided real-time visibility into device health, usage patterns, and security status, allowing the district to identify issues and address them quickly. Automated remediation helped maintain device compliance and reduce risk exposure. Most importantly, the district experienced fewer disruptions to teaching and learning, even under challenging circumstances.

This real-world example illustrates how modern device management can support equitable learning by ensuring that all students have secure, reliable access to educational tools.
