Higher education administrators face the challenges of deploying technologically advanced solutions to campuses while also being mindful of protecting institutional data. Windows 11 Pro devices ship with advanced security features and can be deployed faster. Administrators can apply Zero Trust principles, simplify endpoint management, and utilize AI-driven insights—making Windows 11 Pro devices a natural choice for academic institutions.

Microsoft Intune is a cloud-based service that helps administrators manage deployed devices and apps. With Microsoft Intune, IT administrators can ensure that faculty devices remain compliant and up to date. With Intune, IT administrators can:

- Manage users and devices.
- Simplify app management.
- Automate policy deployment.
- Use self-service features.
- Integrate with mobile threat defense.
- Enable advanced endpoint management and security.
- Use Microsoft Copilot in Intune for AI-generated analysis.

## Integration with other Microsoft services and apps

Microsoft Intune integrates with other Microsoft products and services that focus on endpoint management—allowing IT administrators to empower their institutions and enable scalable operations.

- **Windows Autopilot:** Administrators can provision new devices and send these devices directly to staff from an OEM or device provider.
- **Endpoint analytics:** Helps identify policies or hardware issues that slow down devices while also providing guidance that can help proactively improve end user experiences and reduce help desk tickets.
- **Microsoft 365:** Using Intune, IT administrators can deploy Microsoft 365 apps to managed users and devices.
- **Microsoft Defender for Endpoint:** In Intune, IT administrators can create a service-to-service connection—allowing for the creation of policies that scan files, detect threats, and report threat levels to Microsoft Defender for Endpoint.
- **Windows Autopatch:** A cloud-based service that keeps software current—giving users the latest productivity tools, minimizing on-premises infrastructure, and helps free up IT administrators to focus on other projects.

>[!TIP]
> **Planning for AI-ready endpoints at scale**
> When deploying Copilot+ PCs, IT teams can account for AI-enabled workloads as part of endpoint planning rather than treating as add-on capabilities. Neural Processing Units (NPUs) offload supported AI tasks from the CPU and GPU, allowing Windows 11 to allocate workloads more efficiently while maintaining performance and power efficiency. This architecture supports longer device lifecycles and reduces reliance on cloud-only processing for certain AI scenarios, which can simplify bandwidth planning and align with institutional data residency or privacy requirements.

Microsoft Intune helps IT administrators manage devices at scale while maintaining visibility into compliance across the institution. With these tools, academic institutions have a better understanding of their deployed devices and can keep their faculty and data safe regardless of their preferred workflow.
