## Summarizing emails in Outlook

To start your day, you open your email inbox and calendar in Outlook. Upon reviewing your inbox, you see a message thread with multiple back-and-forth messages. You have a busy day ahead, and reading every message would be time-consuming. Copilot can help reduce time spent reviewing long or complex email threads. Follow these steps to summarize an email thread:

:::image type="content" border="true" source="../media/outlook-summarize.gif" alt-text="Animation showing how to summarize an email thread in Outlook using Copilot.":::

- In either classic Outlook or new Outlook, choose the conversation that you want to summarize.
- Select **Summary by Copilot** (or it might say **Summarize**) at the top of the email thread. Copilot scans the thread to identify key points and create a summary for you.
- The summary appears at the top of the email and may include numbered citations that, when selected, take you to the corresponding email in the thread.
- If you're using new Outlook and the email has attachments (PDF, PowerPoint, or Word), you can select **Summarize a file**. Wait for the summaries to be generated, then review them for a quick view of what you've been sent.

If you're using Outlook on the web, follow these steps:

- In Outlook on the web, choose the conversation that you want.
- Select **Summary by Copilot** (or it might say **Summarize**) at the top of the email thread. Copilot scans the thread to identify key points and create a summary for you.
- The summary appears at the top of the email and may also include numbered citations that, when selected, take you to the corresponding email thread.
- If the email has attachments (PDF, PowerPoint, or Word), you can select **Summarize a file**. Wait for the summaries to be generated, then review them for a quick view of what you've been sent.

## Researching and multitasking

>[!NOTE]
> Copilot+ PCs are designed to support consistent multitasking performance, allowing educators to review summaries, open referenced emails, and switch between Outlook, calendars, and attachments without interrupting workflow.

After teaching your class, it's time to work on research. In your on-campus office, you have a dual monitor set up with a docking station. Connecting this dock to your Copilot+ PC allows you to have a desktop experience that is more comfortable for research, and your Copilot+ PC provides enough power to run academic, productivity, and AI apps simultaneously.

Let's review the steps for setting up your multimonitor setup:

- Make sure your cables are properly connected to your docking station.
- Check for Windows updates. To check for updates, select **Start** then search for **Settings**. Select **Settings** ➡️ **Windows Update** ➡️ **Check for updates**. Next, you'll rearrange your displays once Windows detects that more than one display is connected.
- **Identify a display:** Select **Settings** ➡️**System** ➡️ **Display** ➡️ **Identify**. A number appears on the screen it's assigned to.
- **Detect a display:** If your display is plugged in but not identified in Settings, select **Start** ➡️ **Settings** ➡️ **System** ➡️ **Display** ➡️ **Multiple displays** ➡️ **Detect**.
- **Arrange your displays:** You can arrange the layout of your multiple displays. This is helpful if you want your mouse to move smoothly across the displays—matching your office setup. In **Display** settings, select and drag the display to where you want. When you're happy with the layout, select **Apply**.

Now that your monitors are set up, you can start working on your research. Windows 11 Pro PCs devices feature powerful processors to handle multitasking across multiple applications. To run these simultaneously, a great tool to use is Snap Layouts—allowing you to transition from one app to the next without disrupting your workflow.

:::image type="content" border="true" source="../media/windows-snap-layouts.gif" alt-text="Animation showing how to use Snap Layouts in Windows 11.":::

Snap allows you to quickly resize and position windows on your screen by dragging them to the edges or corners. This helps you work more efficiently by arranging multiple windows to maximize your screen space. Here are the ways that you can use Snap:

- **Snap to the side:** drag a window to the left or right edge of the screen to snap it to that side.
- **Snap to corner:** drag a window to a corner to snap it to that quarter of the screen.
- **Maximize vertically:** drag a window to the top of the screen to expand it vertically, filling the entire height while maintaining its current width.

Snap Assist is a feature in Snap that makes it even easier to organize your windows and workflow. When you snap a window to one side of the screen, Snap Assist will display thumbnails of your other open windows, allowing you to quickly choose which window to snap to the other side. Here's how to use Snap Assist:

- **Snap the first window:** select and drag a window to the edge of the screen until a translucent overlay appears. Release the window to snap it into place.
- **Snap the second window:** select another open window from the thumbnails displayed by Snap Assist to snap it to the other half of the screen.

With the multimonitor setup and Snap Layouts, you can utilize the power of your Copilot+ PC and complete your research more efficiently.

## Windows Studio Effects

:::image type="content" border="true" source="../media/windows-studio-effects.png" alt-text="Screenshot of Windows Studio Effects settings.":::

After your research, the next part of your day involves jumping on a call to speak with your research collaborators. In this session, you'll be leading the conversation so it's important that you look and sound your best.

With Copilot+ PCs, you can use Windows Studio Effects to stand out on calls. Windows Studio Effects applies AI models built by Microsoft and compiled for devices with an NPU (Neural Processing Unit) to deliver high-fidelity, battery-friendly AI effects. These reduce the burden on the device CPU and GPU and provide a trusted Microsoft AI experience that scales across the entire Windows ecosystem for any compatible device. Here's how to configure this feature on your Copilot+ PC:

- Open **Settings** > **Bluetooth & devices** > **Cameras**, then select from the list of Connected cameras and open the settings for that camera.
- You'll see a preview of your front-facing camera display and the Camera Effects listed under it.
- Select the Windows Studio Effects that you would like to enable. Some of these have more settings that can be configured. For instance, Background Effects enable you to choose between Standard blur or Portrait blur.
