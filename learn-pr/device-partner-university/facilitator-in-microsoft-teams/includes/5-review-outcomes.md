Facilitator helps capture information during a meeting, but the value of that information depends on how it's reviewed and used afterward. Meeting notes, decisions, and action items provide participants with a shared record of what was discussed and what needs to happen next.

Reviewing meeting outcomes helps ensure that important information is accurate, responsibilities are clear, and teams remain aligned after the meeting concludes.

## Scenario: Following up after the meeting

A week after the planning session, the marketing team reconvenes to review progress on campaign activities. Team members need to confirm responsibilities, revisit decisions, and verify that important tasks are moving forward.

Because meeting notes, decisions, and action items were captured during the discussion, participants can quickly review what was agreed upon and continue collaborating without recreating the conversation from memory.

## Review collaborative notes

Collaborative notes provide a shared summary of the meeting discussion. Participants can use these notes to revisit topics, confirm details, and clarify any points that may require additional context.

Reviewing notes after a meeting can help participants refresh their understanding of the conversation and verify that important information was captured correctly.

Because notes are shared, they can also serve as a common reference point for participants who need to revisit the discussion later.

## Validate decisions

Meetings often result in decisions that influence projects, priorities, or future work. Reviewing documented decisions helps confirm that meeting outcomes accurately reflect what participants agreed upon.

When reviewing decisions, consider whether:

- The decision is clearly documented.
- Participants share a common understanding of the outcome.
- Additional context or clarification is needed.

Validating decisions after a meeting can help reduce misunderstandings and improve alignment across teams.

## Confirm action items

Action items help turn meeting discussions into measurable next steps. Reviewing action items allows participants to verify responsibilities and understand what work needs to be completed.

When reviewing action items, confirm that:

- Responsibilities are clearly assigned.
- Tasks accurately reflect the discussion.
- Expectations and timelines are understood by participants.

Clear action items can help teams maintain momentum and support accountability after the meeting.

## Use meeting outcomes to support collaboration

Meeting outcomes are most valuable when they remain accessible to participants after the discussion ends.

Facilitator helps organize notes, decisions, and action items in a shared location, making it easier for participants to reference meeting information as work progresses.

Teams can use this shared record to:

- Review previous discussions.
- Track progress on follow-up work.
- Confirm decisions and priorities.
- Maintain alignment across participants.

Having a common source of information helps reduce the need to reconstruct discussions from memory or search through multiple sets of personal notes.

## Maintain a shared source of truth

As projects evolve, teams often need to revisit information from previous meetings. A shared record of meeting outcomes helps establish a consistent source of truth that participants can reference over time.

By reviewing and validating meeting content, participants can help ensure that Facilitator-generated notes, decisions, and action items remain accurate, useful, and actionable.
