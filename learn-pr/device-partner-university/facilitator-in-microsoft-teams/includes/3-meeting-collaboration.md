Meetings help teams share information, make decisions, and coordinate work. However, even productive discussions can lose momentum when important details aren't consistently captured or when participants leave with different understandings of what was discussed.

Facilitator helps address these challenges by creating a shared workspace for meeting notes, decisions, and action items. Instead of relying on one participant to document the conversation, Facilitator helps capture key information as the meeting progresses, giving participants a common reference point throughout the discussion.

## Common meeting challenges

| Challenge | How Facilitator helps |
|------------|----------------------|
| Participants split attention between discussion and note-taking. | Generates collaborative notes that participants can review together. |
| Important decisions aren't consistently documented. | Helps capture and organize decisions discussed during the meeting. |
| Action items can be overlooked after meetings. | Helps identify and track follow-up tasks and responsibilities. |
| Participants leave with different understandings of outcomes. | Creates a shared record of meeting notes, decisions, and action items. |

## Stay focused on the conversation

Many meeting participants balance two responsibilities at the same time: contributing to the discussion and documenting what happens during the meeting. Switching between listening, participating, and note-taking can make it difficult to remain fully engaged.

Facilitator helps reduce this burden by generating collaborative notes during the meeting. Participants can spend more time focusing on the discussion while still maintaining visibility into important information being captured. These notes are shared within the meeting experience, allowing participants to view and contribute to the same set of notes in real time.

Because notes are shared, everyone can review and contribute to the same record of the conversation rather than maintaining separate versions of meeting notes.

## Scenario: Staying aligned during planning and turning discussion into action

During the product launch meeting, the team discusses social media content, email campaigns, and event planning. Rather than dividing attention between participating and taking notes, team members can review collaborative notes generated during the meeting.

This shared view helps everyone stay aligned on what has been discussed and what still needs attention. By documenting next steps alongside meeting notes and decisions, Facilitator helps create a more complete picture of meeting outcomes.

As the meeting concludes, several follow-up tasks are identified. One team member is responsible for creating campaign assets, another needs to coordinate with external partners, and a third is preparing launch communications.

By capturing these action items during the meeting, Facilitator helps ensure responsibilities remain visible and connected to the original discussion.

## Improve visibility into decisions

Meetings often result in decisions that affect projects, processes, or next steps. If those decisions aren't documented clearly, teams may need to revisit discussions later to confirm what was agreed upon.

Facilitator can help identify and capture decisions discussed during the meeting. By creating a visible record of decisions, participants can more easily reference meeting outcomes and maintain alignment after the meeting concludes.

This shared visibility helps reduce confusion and provides a consistent source of information for meeting participants.

## Capture and track action items

Discussions frequently generate follow-up tasks, but action items can sometimes become disconnected from the meeting where they originated.

Facilitator can help capture action items as they're discussed, making it easier for participants to understand what work needs to be completed and who is responsible for completing it.

By documenting next steps alongside meeting notes and decisions, Facilitator can help create a more complete picture of meeting outcomes.

## Create a shared understanding

Participants can occasionally leave a meeting with different interpretations of priorities, responsibilities, or outcomes. A shared record of notes, decisions, and action items helps establish a common understanding among participants.

Facilitator supports collaboration by providing a central location where participants can review meeting content together. This shared visibility helps teams stay aligned before, during, and after meetings.

## Support more effective follow-through

Successful meetings don't end when the conversation concludes. Teams often needs to revisit decisions, complete action items, and continue collaborating after the meeting.

By organizing meeting outcomes in a shared location, Facilitator helps participants:

- Review key discussion points after the meeting.
- Reference documented decisions.
- Track action items and responsibilities.
- Maintain alignment on priorities and next steps.

Knowing how Facilitator supports collaboration is helpful, but seeing it in action provides a better understanding of how it fits into the meeting experience.
