At the end of your workday, it's important to close loops and set up tomorrow for success. Using Copilot in Microsoft 365 apps and Copilot+ PC features, you can prepare for tomorrow—optimizing your efficiency. Let's explore how.

## Daily recap in Outlook and Teams

:::image type="content" border="true" source="../media/copilot-outlook.png" alt-text="Screenshot of Copilot pane and prompt box in Outlook.":::

At the end of your day, it's crucial to see where you stand. To better understand what has been completed, what still needs to be done, and what are your priorities heading into the next day. Utilizing Copilot in either Teams or Outlook helps you formulate a daily recap. First, let's open Copilot from within these applications:

**In Teams:** To open Copilot in Teams, select the **Chat** icon on the left side of the applications, then select **Copilot** to open the Copilot pane.

**In Outlook:** To open Copilot in Outlook, navigate to the **Home** tab. Then select the **Copilot** icon in the toolbar.

Now that you have the prompt box open, enter the following prompt:

*"Draft my end of day update for leadership: accomplishments, decisions, open risks, and top three priorities for tomorrow."*

This prompt helps you close the loop on your completed tasks while neatly creating a list of priorities to be picked up the following day for leadership.

## Scheduling and focus support

Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat is at the center of Microsoft 365 Copilot. With a Microsoft 365 Copilot subscription, you can enable Copilot to incorporate your work content, such as chats, emails, and files. Here's how to use Copilot Chat:

1. Go to [Copilot Chat](https://m365.cloud.microsoft/chat) and sign in with your Microsoft 365 work account.
2. In Copilot Chat, write a prompt in the **Message Copilot** compose box. Select **+ Add content** if you want to upload files and images to your prompt that Copilot Chat can reference when generating a response.
3. When you're satisfied with your prompt select **Send**.

Once you have the prompt box open, enter the following prompt:

*"Book 90 minutes of focused work 9:00-10:30 tomorrow for creative review; add prep notes and invite the design lead"*

For marketing professionals, this prompt helps to quickly schedule focused work, prepare context, and coordinate stakeholders in one step—reducing administrative effort and improving consistency in reporting and process reviews. This is useful when planning the next day or blocking review time.

>[!NOTE]
> When processing calendar requests, Copilot Chat creates a meeting draft or invitation link for your review rather than booking the event automatically. Changes require user confirmation before being finalized. Additionally, stakeholder identification depends on your active organizational directory permissions and existing contacts.

## Get ready for tomorrow with Agent in Settings

With Agent in Settings on your Copilot+ PC, you can use natural language to return your device to common settings so that it's ready for the next day without having to dig through menus. The most common ways to access Agent in Settings are:

- Press **Windows key** + **I**.
- Navigate to the **Settings app** through the **Start Menu**.

Now that Settings are open, do the following:

1. In the top search bar, type: *"Switch back to standard notifications"* and *"Re-enable Teams banners."*
2. As you type, the agent surfaces relevant Don't Disturb and Notification settings.
3. Select **Apply** under the search box to accept the recommended change.

Imagine you've wrapped up a long afternoon of back-to-back client presentations and had enabled Don't Disturb to block out interruptions. Once the meetings end, you need to quickly get back in the loop. By using Agent in Settings with natural language to quickly reset device settings, you help restore notifications and ensure your PC is ready for the next work session with minimal effort.

>[!IMPORTANT]
> Agent in Settings is only available in the following languages: English, French, German, Hindi, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Portuguese, Spanish, and Chinese (simplified). Additionally, this feature is available in all countries/regions except China.
