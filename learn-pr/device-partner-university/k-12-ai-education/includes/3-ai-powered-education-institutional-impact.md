As a new tool in the hands of instructors, students, and administrators, AI is already reshaping education. Here's how AI-powered Microsoft Education solutions can enhance instructional quality and create more personalized learning experiences.

## AI for educators

Educators spend a lot of time designing lessons, creating instructional materials, writing rubrics, and preparing assessments. [Teach in the Microsoft 365 Copilot app](https://support.microsoft.com/topic/teach-in-the-microsoft-365-copilot-app-c4b05fdd-527f-4f85-9775-afb0781a9178) can help teachers prepare high quality instruction in less time, as its AI-powered tools assist in creating lesson plans and drafting materials like quizzes and rubrics.

Rather than spending hours reviewing educational standards documentation and lessons in progress, Copilot can help teachers build drafts that are aligned to learning requirements and objectives. Teachers can also request differentiated versions of a single piece of content and instructional strategies for how to deliver them.

They can take lessons and assignments they've already created for previous classes and update them based on reading level or the needs of individual learners, then create quizzes to evaluate progress. Grading time—which is likely a substantial part of the [54 hours the average teacher works per week](https://www.edweek.org/research-center/reports/teaching-profession-in-crisis-national-teacher-survey)—is also reduced when teachers use AI ready devices and tools to generate feedback then verify their accuracy.

With these AI-assisted workflows, educators spend more time engaging students and less time doing repetitive work.

## AI for students

Students benefit from AI-enabled devices that offer targeted support while adapting to their needs. That's why the [Study and Learn agent in Microsoft 365 Copilot](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/blog/educationblog/ai-powered-teaching-and-learning-for-all-microsoft-365-education-customers/4425282) offers structured guidance, multimodal explanations, and activities that help learners deepen understanding. Meanwhile, Learning Activities such as digital flashcards, matching games, and fill in the blank exercises make practice more engaging.

Instead of waiting for the next free moment in class to understand whether their understanding is correct, students receive feedback when they need it—right as they're practicing a skill, completing an activity, or reviewing a concept. This immediacy helps them correct misunderstandings early, which is especially valuable for learners who may hesitate to ask questions in class.

These tools also give students the chance to build confidence through varied, interactive activities that strengthen comprehension from multiple angles. Whether they're reviewing content with flashcards, filling in the blanks to reinforce foundational knowledge, or matching key concepts to deepen retention, each activity provides another pathway to understanding—all with instant support and feedback even outside of the classroom.

## AI for school admins

Administrators align people, processes, and resources to support student success. To do that, they need the right information—and speedy ways to access and process it.

With AI‑powered tools like [Power BI](https://www.microsoft.com/power-platform/products/power-bi/), administrators can synthesize insights across instruction, attendance, engagement, and more. Instead of manually piecing together information from multiple systems, leaders can get assistance from AI to identify trends and opportunities for targeted interventions. That way, potential issues can be addressed before they become agenda items at the next educational authority meeting.

AI also helps admins cut through time-consuming work to focus on outcomes. They can request quick scenario breakdowns, comparisons across schools, or even summaries of compliance documentation, reclaiming time to focus on strategic initiatives rather than administrative overhead. AI can even help leaders understand how new instructional tools are used across classrooms, so they can ensure they're being used for everyone's benefit.

This shift—from reactive management to proactive, insight‑driven leadership—positions administrators to guide their institutions through modernization with clarity, confidence, and sustainability.

## Potential institutional benefits for K-12

AI's utility goes beyond individual classrooms. Here are some of the biggest impacts it may have on school systems:

- **Consistency**: AI-assisted content creation helps classrooms have access to high quality materials.
- **Scalability:** Instructional improvements can enable transformations for single classrooms, entire grade levels, and schools.
- **Equity:** Students can receive more differentiated support to meet their unique needs.
- **Efficiency:** Teacher workload and administrative pressures may be reduced.

By understanding these concepts and how they enable institutional priorities, leaders can start planning for how to implement AI tools at scale.
