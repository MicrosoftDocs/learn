Choosing the right Windows edition starts with understanding organizational needs rather than comparing features in isolation. In this module, you explored how **Windows 11 Pro** and **Windows 11 Enterprise** are designed to support different operational environments, management approaches, and IT priorities.

You began by examining a common device deployment scenario and identifying the role each Windows edition plays within a modern business environment. From there, you analyzed key **decision criteria**, including security and compliance requirements, device and identity management needs, organizational scale, and governance expectations.

By applying those criteria to real-world scenarios, you saw:

- When **Windows 11 Pro** aligns well with organizations that prioritize flexibility, manageable administration, and business-ready capabilities without unnecessary operational complexity.
- When **Windows 11 Enterprise** is better suited for organizations that require advanced security, centralized management, and consistent governance across large or complex environments.

The goal of this module wasn't to teach how to configure or deploy Windows devices, but to help you make informed and defensible Windows edition decisions based on organizational context.

With this decision framework, you can now evaluate Windows edition choices more effectively and align device planning decisions to operational requirements, management strategies, and long-term organizational needs.
