## Applying the decision criteria

When evaluated against the decision criteria introduced earlier, this scenario aligns well with **Windows 11 Enterprise**.

### Security and compliance requirements

The organization requires advanced security controls to help protect sensitive information and reduce risk across devices. Compliance obligations or internal security policies require a higher level of consistency, visibility, and enforcement across the environment.

Windows 11 Enterprise is designed to support organizations that need advanced security and governance capabilities as part of a broader enterprise security strategy.

### Device and identity management needs

Devices are managed through a centralized IT model where consistent policy application and administrative control are critical. The organization must manage users and devices at scale, often across multiple departments, teams, or geographic locations.

Windows 11 Enterprise supports this centralized management approach, making it well suited for organizations with complex operational structures and enterprise-wide device management needs.

### Organizational scale and complexity

The organization supports a large or highly distributed user base, increasing the importance of standardized configurations and predictable device behavior. As scale and operational complexity increase, the ability to deploy, secure, and manage devices consistently becomes more important.

Windows 11 Enterprise is designed to support these large-scale operational requirements while helping organizations maintain consistency across users, devices, and environments.

### Operational and governance requirements

Formal governance models and standardized operational policies are central to the organization's IT strategy. Devices must align closely with organizational controls, long-term management practices, and enterprise-wide security expectations.

Windows 11 Enterprise supports environments where governance, consistency, and centralized administrative control are key operational priorities.

## Why Windows 11 Enterprise aligns well with this environment

Organizations with similar operational requirements often prioritize:

- **Centralized device management across large environments**
- **Advanced security and compliance capabilities**
- **Standardized governance and policy enforcement**
- **Consistent administrative control across users and devices**
- **Scalable management for complex operational structures**

Windows 11 Enterprise supports these priorities while helping organizations maintain consistency, security, and manageability at scale.

## Why Windows 11 Pro may not fully align with this scenario

Windows 11 Pro supports business-ready security and management capabilities, but it's generally intended for environments with less operational complexity and more flexible management approaches.

In this scenario, the organization's scale, governance requirements, and advanced security expectations require more centralized management and standardized administrative controls than are typically associated with smaller or moderately managed environments.

As a result, Windows 11 Enterprise more closely aligns with the organization's operational model, governance priorities, and enterprise-wide management needs.

## Decision outcome

Based on the organization's advanced security requirements, centralized management model, operational scale, and governance priorities, **Windows 11 Enterprise** is the best fit for this scenario.

It provides the level of consistency, scalability, and administrative control required to support complex organizational environments while helping IT teams maintain standardized security and management practices across the enterprise.
