Organizations deploy Windows devices to support a wide range of users, roles, and business needs. Some environments prioritize simplicity, flexibility, and baseline security, while others require advanced controls, centralized management, and support for large-scale operations. Choosing the right Windows edition starts with understanding these operational differences before evaluating specific capabilities.

In organizational environments, Windows editions designed for business management and operational needs are typically evaluated differently than editions intended primarily for personal use.

In this scenario, you're responsible for recommending a Windows edition as part of an organization's device strategy. The organization is modernizing its Windows device deployment to improve productivity, strengthen security, and support future growth. Devices are used by information workers across multiple departments, and IT teams are responsible for ensuring those devices remain secure, compliant, and manageable throughout their lifecycle.

The organization also supports a mix of workstyles and operational requirements. Some employees primarily work from a central office, while others work remotely or in hybrid environments. Devices must support collaboration, secure access to business resources, and consistent user experiences across different locations and teams.

At the same time, organizational priorities continue to evolve. Leadership teams want to reduce operational complexity while maintaining strong security standards. IT administrators must balance usability and flexibility with the need to manage updates, enforce policies, and protect organizational data. As organizations grow, those requirements often become more complex and may require additional management and security capabilities.

As you progress through this module, you'll evaluate how factors such as organizational size, security expectations, management requirements, and deployment scale can influence edition selection. You'll also consider how existing IT processes, device management strategies, and long-term operational goals can affect which Windows edition is the best fit.

This scenario is intentionally broad so it can reflect common real-world environments, ranging from smaller organizations with limited IT resources to larger organizations with centralized management and compliance needs.

In the following units, you'll use this shared scenario as a decision framework to:

- Examine the available Windows edition options.
- Identify the decision criteria that matter most.
- Apply those criteria to determine when Windows 11 Pro or Windows 11 Enterprise is the best fit.

This decision framework helps focus Windows edition selection on organizational requirements and operational needs rather than comparing features in isolation.
