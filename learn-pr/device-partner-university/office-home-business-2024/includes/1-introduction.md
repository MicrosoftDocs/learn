Imagine you're choosing a Microsoft productivity suite for yourself or for a small business. Perhaps you're starting a new venture that needs fundamental tools to build with, or perhaps you're reassessing your current solutions as your needs evolve.

Either way, you know you need to evaluate core productivity apps—tools for writing documents, analyzing data, creating presentations, managing email, and keeping notes.

What's less obvious is that choosing Office today also means choosing **how your productivity environment behaves over time**.

Some Office offerings emphasize:

- ongoing feature updates
- cloud-connected services
- built-in collaboration
- in some plans, AI-assisted workflows that can speed up common tasks

Other offerings emphasize:

- a stable, locally installed experience
- predictable ownership
- a fixed feature set

Neither approach is inherently better. The right choice depends on your priorities, especially:

- **how much change** you want over time
- how much you want your **productivity tools tied to services**
- and whether you view AI features as a **meaningful benefit** or an optional extra

## Learning objective

By the end of this module, you'll be able to:

- **Choose** the Office productivity option that best fits your needs based on product model, change tolerance, service dependency, AI benefits, and deployment style.
