As you learned in the previous unit, Surface Management Portal is accessed through the Intune Admin Center while the Surface Support Portal is accessed through the Microsoft 365 Admin Center. In this unit, you'll learn how to access each portal.

## Access Surface Management Portal

Because Surface Management Portal is accessed through the Intune Admin Center, an Intune subscription is required to access the service. If you aren't subscribed to Intune, follow these steps:

- Visit the [Intune product page](https://www.microsoft.com/security/business/endpoint-management/microsoft-intune) to learn more about the service.
- Select **Start a free trial** to begin the sign-up process.
- Follow the prompts to create an account and choose a [subscription plan](https://www.microsoft.com/security/business/microsoft-intune-pricing) that fits your organization's needs.
- Complete the setup wizard to configure your Intune environment, adding users and setting up device policies as needed.
- While Intune supports a wide range of devices, you must [enroll at least one Surface device](/mem/intune/user-help/enroll-windows-10-device) to access the Surface Management Portal.

Once signed into Intune, follow these steps to access the Surface Management Portal:

- Sign in to the **Microsoft Intune Admin Center**.
- Select **All services**.
- Select **Surface Management Portal**.

## Access Surface Support Portal

To onboard to Surface Support Portal, you must first validate your organization's Microsoft tenant. Follow these instructions to create a tenant and ensure seamless access to Surface Support Portal via the Microsoft 365 Admin Center:

- Check if your organization has an existing tenant.
- Validate your organization's email and domain.
- Set up a new account for your organization.
- Verify your identity.
- Add your credentials.
- Add payment details.
- Access the Microsoft 365 Admin Center.

:::image type="content" border="true" source="../media/surface-support-portal-access.png" alt-text="Screenshot of accessing Surface Support Portal through Microsoft 365 Admin Center.":::

By embedding Surface-specific management and support capabilities into these existing admin experiences, the portals allow IT teams to manage warranties, monitor device health, and submit or track support requests within familiar workflows.
