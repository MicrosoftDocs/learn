Surface portals provide IT administrators with a centralized solution to manage and monitor Surface devices at scale, with insights and visibility into work environments. With Surface portals, you can do the following:

- Centralize management of device warranties and support requests.
- Initiate single or bulk warranty claims.
- Gain insights into warranty status and claims in a single view.
- Monitor the health of all Surface devices in an organization and get notifications about any unusual device status, which can help support more efficient IT operations.
- Access a unified interface for Surface device management tasks.

For organizations that have deployed Surface devices, there are two different portals that they can utilize to manage their fleet of devices: Surface Management Portal and Surface Support Portal. Surface Management Portal is for commercial users that manage tenants via Microsoft Intune to handle service requests for their devices. Surface Support Portal is for Microsoft 365 Business users to handle their service requests on their deployed Surface devices. Let's explore both portals in more detail.

## Surface Management Portal

:::image type="content" border="true" source="../media/surface-management-portal-access.png" alt-text="Screenshot of accessing Surface Management Portal through Microsoft Intune Admin Center.":::

Located within the Microsoft Intune Admin Center, Surface Management Portal pulls together vital data and insights on enrolled Surface devices in one place. It offers end-to-end visibility, allowing issues to be addressed before they escalate. Management Portal is a solution for IT admins that keeps necessary information accessible, reducing complexity and confusion when managing deployed devices.

With Surface Management Portal IT admins can:

- Gain insights into device compliance, support activity, applicable warranty or protection plan coverage, and carbon emission estimates.
- Monitor the status of each device, including applicable warranty or protection plan expirations and active support requests.
- Centralize Surface-specific device administration in a single environment.
- Automatically access comprehensive information from Intune-enrolled Surface devices, which flows into the Surface Management Portal when users sign in for the first time.

Surface Management Portal may integrate with other Microsoft tools that provide more insights. These integrations help administrators analyze device status and respond to issues more efficiently.

Surface Management Portal enables IT administrators to manage the warranty status of each device to stay on top of expiring warranties and out-of-coverage devices. The portal enables the following to be viewed quickly:

- Devices within the warranty period.
- Devices that are expiring soon.
- Devices out of warranty.
- Devices eligible for optional coverage.

Surface Management Portal is available to those who use Microsoft Intune Admin Center and have at least one Surface device currently enrolled through Intune.

## Surface Support Portal

:::image type="content" border="true" source="../media/surface-support-portal-service.png" alt-text="Screenshot of Surface Support Portal service page.":::

The Surface Support Portal simplifies device support and service management for businesses. With Surface Support Portal, users benefit from:

- Enhanced security with authentication processes to prevent unauthorized access.
- Access to qualified Surface technicians via chat or phone.
- The ability to track complete audit history of exchanges, repairs, and technical support.
- Detailed warranty status, entitlements, and eligibility for advanced warranty programs.

Surface Support Portal is accessed through the Microsoft 365 Admin Center and is used to manage Surface service and support requests. Access to the portal doesn't require a subscription; however, a valid Microsoft tenant is required. Surface Support Portal offers businesses the following benefits:

- **Simplify management:** Streamline device support and reduce the time needed to manage a Surface device fleet with centralized tools. A single portal helps track repairs, monitor device warranty, and eliminate the need to switch between multiple platforms or tools for different tasks.
- **Monitor and manage:** Gain full visibility into support requests with Microsoft. Ticket status can be tracked, and shipping labels downloaded, allowing IT administrators to stay updated on exchange serial numbers and maintain accurate inventory records.
- **Bulk device repair and exchange orders:** Can handle up to 100 devices in a single service order workflow, saving time and effort by processing hardware repairs, battery replacements, damage claims, and related tasks—all while accommodating various warranty types and service agreements.
- **Enhanced security and efficiency:** Secure access to devices, service, and support using Microsoft 365 authentication and validation for added protection.
- **Maximize budget and resources:** Quickly identify which devices are covered under warranty to avoid unnecessary expenses and delays for service and support.
- **AI-enabled guided experiences:** Utilize AI for problem-specific recommendations and step-by-step troubleshooting guidance to help Surface users resolve common technical issues.

>[!IMPORTANT]
> Surface Management Portal is integrated into the Microsoft Intune Admin Center while Surface Support Portal is accessed through the Microsoft 365 Admin Center.
