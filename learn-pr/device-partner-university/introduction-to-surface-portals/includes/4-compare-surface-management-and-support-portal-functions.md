While both the Surface Management and Surface Support Portals offer users a way to manage enrolled devices, their primary purpose differs slightly. Despite differing purposes, there's some functional overlap.

Surface Management Portal is management-led and focused on ongoing device administration for Intune-enrolled Surface devices. Its primary purpose is to help IT administrators monitor and manage Surface devices within the Intune environment. Surface Management Portal enables IT admins to:

- View device health and compliance for Intune-enrolled Surface devices.
- Access Surface-specific insights.
- View warranty information for enrolled devices.
- Initiate service or support actions for Intune-enrolled devices.
- Manage Surface devices as part of Microsoft Intune workflows.
- Provide admins with carbon emission-related insights.

Surface Support Portal is support-led and focused on service and repair workflows, regardless of Intune enrollment. Its primary purpose is to help admins request, track, and manage support and service actions. Surface Support Portal enables admins to:

- Create repair or replacement service orders.
- Create support requests.
- Track the status of service and support requests.
- Manage shipping and service-related details.
- Submit service requests even when devices aren't enrolled in Intune.
- Enable users to create device Autopilot registration requests.
