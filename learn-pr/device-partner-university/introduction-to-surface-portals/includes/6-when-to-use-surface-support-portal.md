These are the typical scenarios for utilizing Surface Support Portal:

- **Creating repair or replacement orders:** Surface Support Portal supports individual and bulk service orders, allowing administrators to efficiently manage repairs, exchanges, battery replacements, and damage claims across multiple devices.
- **Opening Surface support cases:** Administrators can open formal support cases to receive assistance from qualified Surface technicians via chat or phone. These cases are used when troubleshooting is required, when an issue can't be resolved through self-help, or when guidance is needed for hardware, warranty, or service-related concerns.
- **Tracking service and support request status:** Once a repair, replacement, or support request is submitted, the Surface Support Portal allows administrators to monitor its progress end to end. This includes viewing ticket status, accessing shipping details and labels, reviewing repair or exchange history, and tracking newly issued serial numbers for replacement devices.
- **Managing service lifecycle activities:** Surface Support Portal provides a centralized view of the full-service lifecycle for Surface devices, including past and active repairs, exchanges, and support interactions. This ensures consistent oversight of device service activity, even for devices that aren't enrolled in Intune.

>[!TIP]
> If your task begins with "I need to request, manage, or track support," or the device isn't enrolled in Intune, start with Surface Support Portal.

## Scenario

Imagine you're an IT administrator and one of the Surface devices in your organization's fleet is physically damaged and isn't enrolled in Intune. You need to submit a repair request and track its status. What is the next step in your process?

The correct course of action is to use Surface Support Portal to proceed with servicing this device. Surface Support Portal enables administrators to efficiently manage repairs, exchanges, and damage claims across multiple devices. As this task is service-related and the device isn't enrolled in Intune, this is the most efficient course of action.
