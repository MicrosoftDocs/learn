Here are the typical scenarios where utilizing Surface Management Portal applies:

- **Reviewing device health or compliance:** IT administrators can view a device's overall health and compliance status. They can assess whether devices are meeting organizational compliance policies and identify potential issues, such as unusual device stats. This visibility helps reduce downtime and supports more reliable device operations across an organization.
- **Viewing Surface-specific insights:** Beyond general device management data, Surface Management Portal provides insights related to warranty coverage, support activity, and other metrics that help IT admins make more informed decisions about device lifecycle management and long-term planning.
- **Checking warranty information for enrolled devices:** Administrators can quickly see which devices are currently covered, which warranties are nearing expiration, and which are completely out of coverage. This helps to stay ahead of expiring coverage and avoid unexpected repair costs.
- **Initiating a service action directly from a managed device record:** When an issue is identified during device management, service can be initiated through the portal directly since it's integrated directly into the Microsoft Intune Admin Center. This reduces context switching and simplifies the process of moving from issue identification to resolution.

>[!TIP]
> If a task begins with "I need to check or manage an Intune-enrolled Surface device," always start in Surface Management Portal.

## Scenario

Imagine that you're an IT administrator reviewing a Surface device that is enrolled in Intune. You open the Microsoft Intune Admin Center to check the device's health. Upon inspection, you notice that the device may require service. What is your next step?

The correct action is to start in Surface Management Portal to assess the device before initiating any service action. Because of the capability of Surface Management Portal to view Surface-specific insights, you can easily diagnose the issue—helping to expedite the service process.
