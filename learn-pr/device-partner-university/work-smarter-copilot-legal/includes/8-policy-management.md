After meeting with stakeholders, you review compliance information to understand potential issues, identify next steps, and prepare for upcoming reviews. This often requires working through trackers, reports, and other documents, making it difficult to quickly identify gaps, deadlines, and areas that might need attention.

Copilot in Excel and Copilot in Word can help analyze compliance information, summarize findings, and highlight areas for further review.

Copilot can help you:

- Identify missing items or incomplete fields.
- Highlight upcoming deadlines.
- Summarize possible issues, gaps, or areas that might require further review.
- Draft proposed follow-up actions.

Do the following to analyze compliance information:

1. Open the compliance tracker in Microsoft Excel or Word.
2. Select Copilot.
3. Enter the following prompt:
  *“Review this compliance tracker and identify missing fields, upcoming deadlines, and items that might require further legal or compliance review. Summarize proposed follow-up actions and flag assumptions.”*

After reviewing the tracker, use Copilot to turn your findings into a summary for leadership, stakeholders, or compliance teams. Including the audience in your prompt can help Copilot tailor the response accordingly.

Enter the following prompt:
  *“Generate a one-page leadership summary of key issues, proposed actions, open questions, and follow-up activities, and draft a Teams update assigning follow-up actions."*

This helps you move from reviewing compliance information to identifying priorities, responsibilities, and next steps that can be shared with leadership and compliance teams. These findings can also inform policy updates or revisions that require legal review.

**When to use**: Both Copilot in Excel and Copilot in Word can help analyze compliance information and summarize findings. If you're working with structured data, consider using Copilot in Excel. If you're working with written compliance documents or summaries, consider using Copilot in Word. Use Copilot in PowerPoint when you need to present issues, recommendations, and decisions to leadership or other stakeholders.

## Update policies and prepare document revisions

While reviewing compliance information, you identify policies that are no longer up to date. You need to update those policies and prepare revised versions for review.

Sometimes legal teams need to update policies to reflect new regulatory guidance, internal practices, business changes, or compliance requirements. These updates often require careful comparison of source materials, previous policy versions, and required acknowledgments.

Copilot in Word can help create a first draft of proposed policy updates for legal review. For example, it can help you:

- Draft suggested policy changes.
- Highlight sections that might require review.
- Identify acknowledgment language.
- Prepare a revised version for legal approval.

To update a policy document:

1. Open the policy document in Microsoft Word.
2. Select Copilot.
3. Enter the following prompt:
  *"Using only the source materials I provide, draft proposed updates to this policy for legal review. Highlight suggested changes, assumptions, required acknowledgments, and areas that need validation against current regulations and internal practices.”*

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Copilot can help draft policy revisions, but it doesn't independently determine current regulations, compliance requirements, or legal obligations. Always review and validate suggested changes against applicable laws, regulations, internal policies, and organizational requirements before approval or publication.

Copilot can help accelerate the creation of a first draft while making it easier to compare proposed updates against existing requirements and guidance.

You can also use other Copilot+ PC experiences to support the process:

- Click to Do can help refine selected text or tables for clarity and consistency.
- Improved Windows Search can help you locate templates, previous policies, regulatory guidance, or audit materials that support the update.

After reviewing and finalizing policy changes, you can use Copilot in PowerPoint to prepare a presentation that summarizes compliance findings, possible issues, recommendations, and proposed next steps.

> [!TIP]
> Compare Copilot's proposed revisions with the original policy and supporting source materials to verify accuracy and completeness.
