## Refine legal content with Click to Do

:::image type="content" source="../media/click-to-do-text.png" alt-text="Screenshot of Click to Do actions available for selected text during a Teams meeting." lightbox="../media/click-to-do-text-expanded.png":::

You made changes to your contracts as you reviewed the content. Now you need to make sure that information makes sense to other people. Legal content often needs to be adapted for different audiences. A clause might need to be explained in plain language for a business team, or a policy update might need clearer wording before review.

Click to Do on Copilot+ PCs can help you interact with text on your screen without switching between apps. You can select text or tables and access suggestions to rewrite, summarize, or improve clarity. This can help you adjust legal communications more efficiently while reducing time spent moving content between tools.

For example, you might use Click to Do to turn dense contract language into a clearer explanation for a business audience. You could select a clause and use Rewrite to create a version that's easier for stakeholders to understand.

> [!NOTE]
> Availability and capabilities vary by device, market, region, language, character set, and configuration. Some Click to Do actions might invoke services that require a subscription.

Click to Do can help you:

- Improve clarity.
- Increase consistency.
- Prepare business-friendly explanations.
- Refine policy or guidance language without switching apps.

Do the following to use Click to Do for document refinements:

1. Open the document, email, or policy text you want to review.
2. Activate Click to Do by pressing Windows key + mouse click.
3. Select the text or table you want to refine.
4. Choose an available action, such as Rewrite.
5. Review the suggestion before adding it to your document.

This can be especially useful when preparing content for different audiences, such as translating legal concepts into language that business teams can more easily understand.

**When to use**: Use Click to Do when you want to improve or rework existing text without leaving your current app. Use Copilot in Word when you need to create new content, analyze larger documents, or produce a more structured draft.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Always compare suggested rewrites against the original language to confirm that legal meaning and required terminology are preserved. Review Rewrite suggestions before incorporating them into legal documents.

## Draft plain-language guidance for stakeholders

After reviewing a contract, policy, or compliance issue, legal professionals often need to communicate findings to business teams and other stakeholders. These updates need to be clear, actionable, and written in a way that makes sense for a nonlegal audience.

Copilot in Word or Copilot in OneNote can help turn review notes into a plain-language summary that others can understand. This can be useful when explaining legal issues, gaps, or areas for review, open questions, recommended positions, or next steps.

Copilot can help you:

- Summarize key findings.
- Explain legal issues, gaps, or areas for review in plain language.
- Organize open questions and next steps.
- Prepare updates for different audiences.

Do the following to draft stakeholder guidance:

1. Open Microsoft Word or OneNote.
2. Select Copilot.
3. Enter the following prompt:
  *“Draft a plain-language summary for review that explains key legal issues, areas requiring review, recommended positions, open questions, and next steps for business stakeholders.”*

Copilot can help create a first draft that you can review and refine before sharing. This allows you to spend less time starting from a blank page and more time reviewing the content and recommendations.

Clear communication can help stakeholders understand issues, gaps, or areas for legal review, make informed decisions, and align on next steps.

**When to use**: Use Copilot in Word or Copilot in OneNote when you have legal notes, findings, or analysis that need to be turned into clear guidance. Use OneNote when working from notes or collected information and you need help with organizing and structuring your ideas. Use Word when drafting or refining the final document that will be reviewed or shared.

> [!TIP]
> Ask Copilot to tailor the output for a specific audience. For example, you can request an executive summary for leadership, a status update for business teams, or a detailed analysis for members of your legal team.
