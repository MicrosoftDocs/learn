Now that you have successfully either inserted your physical Nano SIM or installed your eSIM profile, you're ready to activate service on your Surface device.

>[!NOTE]
> Service must be activated through your mobile operator before accessing 5G services on your Surface for Business device.

## With a physical nano SIM

After ensuring that your service is activated from your mobile operator and the SIM card is correctly inserted, do the following to activate service on your Surface:

1. Select **Start**.
2. Open **Settings**.
3. Select **Network & internet**.
4. Select **Cellular**.
5. Select **SIM1** from **Use this SIM for cellular data**.

Your SIM is now active and 5G data is ready to be used on your Surface.

## With eSIM

After setting up your eSIM profile, follow these steps to connect to cellular data:

1. Select the **Start** button, then enter **Settings**. Select **Settings** > **Network & internet** > **Cellular** > **eSIM profiles**.
2. Under **Profiles for cellular data**, select the eSIM profile you want, and then select **Use**.
3. Select **Yes** for **This will use cellular data from your data plan and may incur charges. Do you want to continue?** After accepting, you'll be connected to a cellular data network and are ready to go.

>[!TIP]
> You can temporarily turn off cellular data when not in use. To do this:
> 1. Open **Settings**.
> 2. Select **Network & internet** on the left side of the screen.
> 3. Under **Cellular**, select the slider to **Off**.
> 4. Select the slider again if you wish to turn cellular connectivity back on.

## eSIM profile management

If you use eSIM and you have more than one eSIM profile installed on your Surface, you can switch between them to use a different mobile operator and data plan. To do this, follow these steps:

1. Select the **Start** button, then enter **Settings**. Select **Settings** > **Network & internet** > **Cellular** > **eSIM profiles**.
2. Under **Profiles for cellular data**, select the profile you want to stop using, and then select **Stop using**.
3. Select **Yes** or **You'll be disconnected from this cellular network. Continue?**.
4. Select the different profile you want to use, then select **Use**.

If you have an eSIM profile that you don't want to use anymore, you can easily delete it from your Surface for Business device. To do so, do the following:

1. Select the **Start** button, then enter **Settings**. Select **Settings** > **Network & internet** > **Cellular** > **eSIM profiles**.
2. Under **Profiles for cellular data**, select the profile to delete, and then select **Delete**.
3. At the prompt that warns you that the profile will be permanently deleted, select **Yes**.

>[!NOTE]
> If your Surface device is managed by your organization, you might not be able to delete an eSIM profile because of a policy set by your organization. Remember that deleting an eSIM profile doesn't cancel wireless service agreements. To do so, you must contact your mobile service operator.
