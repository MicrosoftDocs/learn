In today's commercial landscape, work is no longer confined to a traditional office desk. Empowering a hybrid workforce requires devices that offer a mix of performance, security, and connectivity from anywhere. Surface for Business devices with 5G bridge the gap between traditional office environments and the field—ensuring that critical data and collaboration tools are available.

But is a 5G-enabled Surface right for you? Let's explore some industry use cases where Surface 5G devices enhance organizational productivity:

- **Frontline and field services:** Technical engineers, utility workers, and field adjusters require constant access to cloud-based diagnostic tools, schematics, and real-time reporting. Surface 5G removes reliance on public Wi-Fi or mobile hotspots, ensuring continuous uptime.
- **Healthcare and home health:** Mobile clinicians and visiting nurses can securely access and update electronic health records directly from a patient's bedside. High bandwidth 5G enables seamless telehealth consultations and transmission of high-resolution medical imaging.
- **Executive and mobile professionals:** Traveling executives and consultants can maintain a secure, high-speed connection between meetings, at airports, or during transit. This ensures uninterrupted productivity and immediate access to sensitive corporate resources via the cloud.
- **Financial and legal services:** Professionals managing highly confidential client data can conduct sensitive transactions and review legal documents remotely without the compliance risks associated with unencrypted public networks.

Next, let's explore a couple of real-world scenarios where 5G on Surface devices help business users accomplish their tasks efficiently.

## Scenario one: Remote infrastructure field engineer

**User profile:** Sarah is a Senior Field Engineer for a regional utility company. Her role involves inspecting remote substations, troubleshooting equipment failures, and uploading massive data logs to Azure for analysis.

**The challenge:** Substation environments are often located far outside standard Wi-Fi ranges. Previously, Sarah had to rely on a separate mobile hotspot, which drained her phone battery, frequently dropped connection during file transfers, and exposed her device to potential security vulnerabilities.

**The solution:** Equipped with a Surface Pro for Business (12th Edition) with 5G, Sarah opens her device right at the job site. The integrated 5G connection allows her to upload diagnostic logs directly to the cloud in seconds. She can collaborate in real-time with video calling on Microsoft Teams, utilizing Windows Studio Effects and 5G bandwidth to ensure that video is crisp and her voice is clear, despite ambient outdoor noise.

## Scenario two: Corporate legal counsel

**User profile:** Marcus is an in-house corporate attorney who splits his time between courthouse chambers, corporate offices, and transit hubs. He frequently reviews high-stakes merger documentation and participates in confidential board meetings on short notice.

**The challenge:** Working from airport lounges or public coffee shops between meetings presents a massive security risk. Public Wi-Fi networks are notorious vectors for cyber-attacks, and corporate compliance forbids Marcus from using them to access sensitive legal files.

**The solution:** Using a Surface Laptop for Business with 5G, Marcus maintains security and speed without friction. By bypassing public Wi-Fi entirely and connecting through a private corporate eSIM profile on the 5G network, Marcus reduces standard network interception risks. This architecture complements the device's Secured-core PC status, protecting sensitive data from the chip to the cloud. With the embedded 5G chip on his Surface, Marcus enjoys the sleek form factor of a commercial laptop without the added bulk or awkwardness of external dongles and connections.
