In this exercise, you'll review a set of short scenarios and identify which concepts from this module are most relevant in each case. The goal isn't to fix a problem or perform a task, but to practice recognizing how authenticity, trust, and risk show up in everyday situations.

Read each scenario and consider which concepts apply most strongly. You don't need to choose a single "right" answer—focus on the reasoning. Then, check the key takeaway to find more to consider.

## Scenario 1: Refurbished laptops

A small business purchases refurbished laptops from a non-Microsoft vendor. The devices run Windows and appear to work normally, but there's limited documentation about who installed the operating system or how.

### Think about:

Which concepts help explain the potential uncertainty in this situation? How could that uncertainty affect how the business operates in the future?

### Key takeaway:

Even when a system appears to work, uncertainty about software authenticity can undermine confidence in the device's origin and integrity—highlighting why genuineness matters beyond surface level functionality.

## Scenario 2: Long-term device planning

An organization plans to standardize on a Windows device platform for at least three years. Leadership wants to limit potential surprises related to reliability, support, and compliance.

### Think about:

How do authenticity and trust affect comprehensive decision making?

### Key takeaway:

Genuine Windows supports trust and predictability over time, making it easier to reason about future outcomes and manage risk.

## Scenario 3: "It works, so it's fine"

An individual uses a personal Windows device in their daily work. It functions as expected and hasn't caused any obvious problems. They assume genuineness doesn't matter if everything works today.

### Think about:

Which concepts from this module challenge that assumption?

### Key takeaway:

This scenario highlights the difference between short term appearance and long-term confidence, showing that the individual might be leaving room for more risks later down the line.
