As organizations adopt AI across devices and services, they need consistent ways to manage how these experiences are configured and used. Organizations can use Microsoft 365 and Microsoft Intune to manage both on-device and cloud-based AI experiences.

When managed through Microsoft Intune, organizations can:

- **Apply policies across devices** to control access, configuration, and usage of AI features  
- **Align AI experiences with compliance requirements** using device and identity-based controls  
- **Configure features like Recall** based on organizational requirements  

These controls allow IT administrators to manage how AI is introduced and used across the organization without requiring separate management tools. Beyond management, organizations also need to align AI usage with broader security frameworks and policies.

## Governing AI experiences

IT administrators can apply policies to:

- Enable or disable features like Recall  
- Configure data retention and storage settings  
- Restrict which apps or websites can be captured  
- Enforce security and compliance requirements  

This ensures that AI-powered experiences align with organizational policies while still delivering productivity benefits.

## Supporting modern security models

These capabilities support broader security approaches, including Zero Trust principles, by enabling:

- Continuous verification of identity and device state  
- Least-privilege access controls  
- Protection of sensitive data across endpoints  

As organizations adopt AI experiences, these controls help ensure that AI capabilities remain secure, governed, and compliant. Together, these management and policy controls help organizations apply consistent governance to AI experiences across devices and users, while supporting compliance and operational requirements.
