When IT tickets are created, the speed of resolution is crucial. When business users create an IT support ticket, it's usually a time-sensitive matter that needs rapid response and resolution. This puts added pressure on IT professionals to diagnose issues quickly and restore service so that work continues to get done efficiently. With Microsoft 365 integration in Office apps and on-device Copilot+ PC AI tools, IT professionals can expedite their process and enable quicker ticket resolution.

## Simplifying processes with Copilot in Office apps

Once the ticket is received, use Copilot in Word or OneNote to issue context consolidation:

- Open the ticket in Word or OneNote depending on your preferred workflow.
- Select the Copilot pane.
- In the prompt box enter the following:

*"Create a one-page incident summary using ticket details, logs, and notes from the morning. Include impact, root cause hypotheses, and next steps."*

>[!NOTE]
> Copilot responses are generated from available document and organizational context. Logs and notes must already be accessible in the document or connected sources to be accessed.

Copilot helps you compile the information you have from the ticket to create an incident summary into one synthesized document, offering a ready-to-share report instead of manually reading through all inputs.

Next, you need to have an incident update for team leadership as part of the documentation and reporting process. This used to be a time-consuming task. But with Copilot in Word or PowerPoint it's more efficient:

- Open Word or PowerPoint depending on your preferred workflow.
- Select the Copilot pane.
- In the prompt box, type the following:

*"Draft a short incident update for leadership: issue overview, mitigation steps taken, current status, and estimated time of completion."*

Copilot helps put all the important updates for this incident in one document that is easily scannable for leadership—enabling you to move forward with resolving this incident.

## Rewrite text with Click to Do

:::image type="content" border="true" source="../media/click-to-do.jpeg" alt-text="Screenshot of the Click to Do feature in Teams on a Copilot+ PC.":::

Consider a situation where you need to share incident notes with an executive audience. The issue that arises is that they may not fully comprehend what is in the notes, instead needing a clear, concise, and nontechnical explanation. Using [Click to Do](https://startwithcopilotpc.campaignhubs.com/p/11) on your Copilot+ PC, this task can be simplified:

- Open the incident report on your PC.
- Highlight the text that you need to share with executives.
- Activate Click to Do by pressing **Windows key** + **mouse click**. You'll see a subtle visual change on screen letting you know that Click to Do is active.
- Click to Do will surface suggested actions. For this example, select **Rewrite**.
- Click to Do will rewrite the text for your audience in easier to understand terms.

>[!TIP]
> Review rewritten content before sharing externally to confirm it's accurate, appropriate for the audience, and aligned with your organization's policies.

## Finding important files with Improved Windows Search

:::image type="content" border="true" source="../media/improved-windows-search.jpeg" alt-text="Screenshot of Improved Windows Search in File Explorer on a Copilot+ PC.":::

Imagine that you are resolving a new IT ticket, but the situation reminds you of a previous incident you resolved. What used to require digging through emails and folders can now easily be retrieved with [Improved Windows Search](https://startwithcopilotpc.campaignhubs.com/p/10) on your Copilot+ PC. Here's how to use it:

- Open Improved Windows Search by selecting the search box on the taskbar or pressing **Windows key** + **S**.
- In the search box, use natural language to describe prior incident reports or scripts that you're looking for, such as "PowerShell script for VPN reset."
- Improved Windows Search surfaces relevant files based on their content, not just their file name.
- If you need to narrow the results down further, select **Filters**, then choose **Documents** to focus on files only.
- Quickly confirm that you have selected the right file by hovering over the result to preview the file before opening it.

>[!NOTE]
> Improved Windows Search supports common file formats, including:
> Word documents
> Excel spreadsheets
> PowerPoint presentations
> Text files
> PDF files
> Image formats including JPEG, PNG, GIF, BMP, and ICO

## Stay in your workflow with Recall (preview)

:::image type="content" border="true" source="../media/recall-timeline.png" alt-text="Screenshot of Recall (preview) timeline on a Copilot+ PC.":::

Imagine that while resolving another ticket, you need to jump back to the dashboard, log view, or documentation you referenced last week. But you're unsure where those files are. With Recall (preview) on your Copilot+ PC, you can use natural language to find what you need without breaking your flow:

- Select the **Recall (preview) icon** on your taskbar and select **Open Recall (preview)**.
- For privacy and security, Windows Hello will verify your identity before showing your snapshot timeline.
- Once Recall (preview) opens, select Timeline to scroll through snapshots of things you've seen or worked on.
- You can also describe what you're looking for to search across apps, files, websites, and your browsing history.
- Select the snapshot that matches what you need.
- From there, you can take quick actions with a click or open the app, document, or site to pick up right where you left off.

>[!NOTE]
> Recall (preview) requires [Windows Hello Enhanced Sign-in Security](/windows-hardware/design/device-experiences/windows-hello-enhanced-sign-in-security). Optimized for select languages (English, Simplified Chinese, French, German, Japanese, and Spanish). Content-based and storage limitations apply.
> Recall (preview) is optional and configurable, to support trust and adoption.
