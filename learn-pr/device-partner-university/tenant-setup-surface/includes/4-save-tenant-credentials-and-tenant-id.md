Now that your tenant has been created, it's time to create your sign-in credentials. You'll set up a username and password for the admin account. This account becomes the Global Admin for your tenant—giving you full access to the Surface Support Portal and the ability to assign access roles to others in your organization. These credentials will be used to log in to your Microsoft tenant. Follow these steps to complete tenant setup:

:::image type="content" border="true" source="../media/tenant-sign-in.png" alt-text="Screenshot of tenant how you'll sign-in page.":::

- Provide sign-in credentials: username, domain name, and password. These credentials will be used to log into your Microsoft tenant—be sure to store them securely.

:::image type="content" border="true" source="../media/add-payment-method.png" alt-text="Screenshot of add payment method page.":::

- Enter credit card information for identity verification. This is only for verification; no charges are applied.

>[!IMPORTANT]
> The credit card provided won't be used for any Surface Support services. It can only be used later if you choose to purchase extra subscriptions, such as Microsoft 365 for Business, Copilot, or other Microsoft services.
> Once your tenant is created, you'll be able to update your credit card and billing information anytime through your Microsoft 365 billing profile.

- At the end of the tenant creation, save the tenant details provided. These details are essential for future access to the tenant.

>[!NOTE]
> Once the tenant has been created, the setup flow continues with multifactor authentication (MFA) configuration. This step provides secure access to the tenant from the start, with prompts to set up the Microsoft Authenticator app. Follow the instructions to download the app, link it to your account, and confirm your identity. With MFA in place, your new tenant is now protected by default.
