Alex has reviewed upcoming assignments, deadlines, and responsibilities.

Before starting any work, Alex wants to create a clear plan for the day.

A history essay draft is due later this week, math homework needs to be completed, and a biology quiz is coming up. Without a plan, it's easy to lose track of priorities or spend too much time deciding what to do next.

## Your task

Help Alex create a structured plan using Microsoft Word.

You'll:

- Capture tasks
- Break larger assignments into manageable steps
- Prioritize what needs attention first
- Use Copilot to refine the plan

## Step 1: Create a task list in Word

:::row:::

:::column span="2":::

Open a new Word document and create a list of everything Alex needs to accomplish.

Include items such as:

- Assignments and homework
- Notes to review
- Study time for quizzes or exams
- Group projects or meetings

Use bullet points to keep the list organized and easy to review.

:::column-end:::

## Step 2: Break larger tasks into smaller steps

Review the task list and identify assignments that might require multiple steps.

For example:

**History essay**

- Create an outline
- Write an introduction
- Add supporting details
- Review and revise

Breaking assignments into smaller actions makes them easier to start and track.

Add these steps beneath each assignment in your document.

## Step 3: Prioritize your work

Now review the list and decide which tasks should be completed first.

Consider:

- Upcoming deadlines
- Estimated time required
- Tasks that need to be completed before other work can begin

Use headings, bold text, or numbered lists in Word to highlight high-priority items.

## Step 4: Use Copilot to refine your plan

With your task list organized, use Copilot in Word to help improve and refine your plan.

To get started:

1. Open your Word document.
1. On the Home tab, select the Copilot button on the ribbon.
   :::image type="content" source="../media/copilot-word-home-ribbon.png" alt-text="Screenshot of Microsoft Word Home tab showing the Copilot button highlighted on the ribbon." lightbox="../media/copilot-word-home-ribbon.png":::
1. Enter a prompt describing the help you need.
   :::image type="content" source="../media/copilot-in-word-draft.png" alt-text="Screenshot of Draft with Copilot pane in Microsoft Word showing a text box to enter a prompt for generating a draft." lightbox="../media/copilot-in-word-draft.png":::
1. Review the results and make any adjustments before using them in your plan.

Copilot can help organize information, suggest priorities, and recommend next steps.

For example, Alex might ask:

- "Organize this task list into a clear daily schedule."
- "Identify which tasks should be completed first based on deadlines."
- "Summarize my tasks into a prioritized plan for today."

After Copilot generates a response, review the suggestions and update your plan as needed.

## Step 5: Finalize your schedule

Review the plan and make any final adjustments.

For example:

- Reorder tasks.
- Add estimated completion times.
- Clarify next steps.
- Remove completed items.

Your document should now provide a clear plan that helps guide your work throughout the day.

## Check your progress

You should now have:

- A complete task list.
- Smaller steps for larger assignments.
- A prioritized plan.
- A schedule for the day.

## Key takeaway

Using Word to organize your tasks—and Copilot to refine your plan—can help you stay focused, manage your time more effectively, and make steady progress throughout the school day.
