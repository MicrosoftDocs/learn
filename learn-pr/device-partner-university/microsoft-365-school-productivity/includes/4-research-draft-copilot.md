With a plan in place, Alex is ready to start working on assignments.

One of Alex's highest-priority assignments is a history essay.

Before starting the first draft, Alex needs to:

- Understand the assignment requirements
- Gather relevant information
- Organize ideas into a clear structure

Taking a few minutes to prepare before writing can help make the drafting process easier and more efficient.

## Why research and planning matter

Starting an assignment without preparation can make writing more difficult.

For example, you may:

- Miss important information
- Struggle to organize your ideas
- Spend more time revising later

By researching and organizing information first, you can build a strong foundation for your assignment and make the writing process more manageable.

## Review the assignment

Alex begins by reviewing the assignment prompt.

Before researching, it's important to understand what the assignment is asking.

Consider questions such as:

- What is the topic?
- What information should be included?
- What is the goal of the assignment?
- Are there any specific requirements or instructions?

Understanding the assignment helps focus your research and identify the information you need to gather.

## Gather and organize information

After reviewing the assignment, Alex begins collecting information and notes.

As you research your topic, look for:

- Key facts and concepts
- Supporting examples
- Information from class materials
- Notes from previous lessons

Microsoft 365 can help keep research materials organized throughout the process.

For example:

- Use OneNote to collect notes, research, and ideas.
- Use Word to organize information and begin preparing your assignment.

Keeping research materials organized makes it easier to find information later and helps create a smoother writing experience.

## Create an outline in Word

Once enough information has been collected, Alex creates an outline in Word.

A simple outline can help organize ideas before writing begins.

For example:

- Introduction: Main idea or argument
- Body paragraph 1: Supporting point
- Body paragraph 2: Supporting point
- Conclusion: Summary of key ideas

An outline provides a roadmap for your assignment and helps keep your writing focused.

## Use Copilot to organize your ideas

After gathering notes and creating an outline, Alex uses Copilot to help organize information and identify key themes.

For example, Alex might ask:

- "Summarize these notes into key points for my essay."
- "Create an outline based on this information."
- "Identify the main ideas I should include in my introduction."

Copilot can help:

- Summarize research and notes
- Identify important themes
- Organize information into a logical structure
- Suggest ways to strengthen an outline

> [!TIP]
> Use Copilot to support your research and planning, but always review the results and ensure they align with your assignment requirements.
> 
> Always follow your institution's policies when using AI tools for academic work.

## Move from planning to writing

At this point, Alex has:

- Reviewed the assignment requirements
- Gathered research and notes
- Organized information
- Created an outline

Instead of starting with a blank page, Alex now has a clear plan for the assignment and is ready to begin writing.

As the draft takes shape, tools such as Copilot and Editor in Word can help refine ideas, improve clarity, and review writing before submission.
