Alex has completed the research process and created an outline for the history essay.

With research notes, supporting information, and a clear structure already in place, it's time to begin writing the first draft.

Instead of starting with a blank page, Alex can use the outline and notes gathered earlier to build the assignment section by section.

## Your task

Help Alex create a first draft using Word and Copilot.

You'll:

- Use an outline to begin writing
- Expand ideas into complete paragraphs
- Use Copilot to refine and strengthen content
- Review and improve the draft

## Step 1: Start writing in Word

Open the Word document containing your outline and research notes.

Begin drafting the introduction.

Focus on:

- Introducing the topic
- Explaining the purpose of the assignment
- Presenting the main idea or argument

Don't worry about making the introduction perfect. The goal is to begin turning ideas into written content.

## Step 2: Expand your outline into paragraphs

Choose one section of the outline and begin developing it into a complete paragraph.

As you write:

- Expand key points into explanations
- Include supporting details and examples
- Connect ideas using complete sentences

Continue building the assignment one section at a time.

Remember that first drafts are meant to capture ideas. You can improve organization and wording later.

## Step 3: Use Copilot to improve specific sections

After creating a draft, use Copilot in Word to improve individual sections of your assignment.

For example, Alex might:

- Rewrite an introduction to make it more engaging
- Expand a paragraph with additional supporting details
- Improve transitions between sections
- Simplify a sentence that is difficult to understand

To get started:

1. Select the text you want to improve.
1. Open Copilot in Word.
1. Enter a prompt describing the change you want to make.
1. Review the suggested content.
1. Update your draft as needed.

For example, Alex might ask:

- "Rewrite this introduction to make it more engaging."
- "Add supporting details to this paragraph."
- "Improve the transition between these sections."
- "Simplify this paragraph while keeping the main idea."

> [!TIP]
> Use Copilot to build on your ideas, but always review the results carefully and ensure the content reflects your understanding and assignment requirements.

## Step 4: Review your draft with Editor

After completing your draft, use Editor in Word to review your writing.

Editor can help identify:

- Spelling mistakes
- Grammar issues
- Punctuation errors
- Opportunities to improve clarity

To review your draft:

1. Open your document in Word.
1. Select Editor from the ribbon in the Home tab.
   
   :::image type="content" source="../media/word-editor-home.png" alt-text="Screenshot of Microsoft Word Home tab showing the Editor button highlighted on the ribbon." lightbox="../media/word-editor-home.png":::

1. Open Editor.
1. Review suggested improvements.

   :::image type="content" source="../media/editor-pane-suggestion.png" alt-text="Screenshot of Editor pane in Microsoft Word showing clarity suggestions with alternative word choices listed." lightbox="../media/editor-pane-suggestion.png":::

1. Decide which suggestions to accept or ignore.

As Alex reviews the essay, Editor highlights a few spelling and grammar issues that can be corrected before submission.

Improving these details can help make your writing clearer and easier to understand.

## Check your progress

You should now have:

- A completed introduction
- At least one developed body paragraph
- Supporting details based on your research
- A stronger draft refined with Copilot

## Key takeaway

Creating a strong assignment starts with a clear plan, organized research, and a structured outline.

Using Word to draft your ideas—and Copilot to refine and strengthen them—helps you write more efficiently while staying focused on your assignment goals.
