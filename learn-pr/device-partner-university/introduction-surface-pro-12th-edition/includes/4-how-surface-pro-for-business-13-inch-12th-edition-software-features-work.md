As a Copilot+ PC, Surface Pro for Business, 13-inch (12th Edition) enables on-device AI experiences that help complete repeatable tasks quicker. Let's learn how to utilize some of these experiences.

## Recall (preview)

Imagine you're working on an important presentation. You remember stumbling across a chart that would be great for a slide, but you forgot where you saw it. In the past, this meant digging through your browser history in the hopes of finding the right page.

:::image type="content" border="true" source="../media/recall-demo.gif" alt-text="Animation demonstrating Recall (preview) on Surface Pro for Business, 13-inch (12th Edition).":::

With Recall (preview) on Surface Pro for Business, 13-inch (12th Edition), you can use an explorable timeline to find the content you remember seeing before. Using semantic powered search, describe how you remember something, and Recall (preview) helps you find previously viewed content. Any photo, link, or message can be a fresh point to continue from.

>[!NOTE]
> To use Recall (preview), you need to opt in to saving snapshots, which are screenshots of your activity. Snapshots and the contextual information derived from them are saved and encrypted on your local hard drive. Recall (preview) doesn't share snapshots or associated data with Microsoft or third parties, nor is it shared between different Windows users on the same device.

If you opt into using Recall (preview), then as you use your Surface, a snapshot of your active screen will be saved every few seconds and when the content of your active window changes. Snapshots are also protected with Windows Hello, so that only the signed in user can access their Recall (preview) content.

To use Recall (preview) your Surface Pro for Business, 13-inch (12th Edition) must meet the following requirements:

- At least 50GB of storage space free.
- Device Encryption or BitLocker enabled.
- Enrolled into Windows Hello Enhanced Sign-In Security with at least one biometric sign-in option enabled to authenticate.

Recall (preview) supports multiple web browsers, and their capabilities include:

- **Microsoft Edge:** filters specified websites and filters private browsing activity.
- **Firefox:** filters specified websites and filters private browsing activity.
- **Opera:** filters specified websites and filters private browsing activity.
- **Google Chrome:** filters specified websites and filters private browsing activity.
- **Chromium-based browsers (version 124 and later):** for Chromium-based browsers not listed above, filters private browsing activity only, doesn't filter specific websites.

To open Recall (preview), use the keyboard shortcut **Windows Key + J**, by selecting Recall from **Start > All Apps**, or select the Recall icon on your taskbar.

>[!NOTE]
> Every time you launch Recall (preview) or change Recall (preview) settings, you'll be asked to confirm that it's you by using Windows Hello. Your saved snapshots and any associated information are encrypted so that only you can access them.

Once you've confirmed your identity, you're taken to your Recall (preview) homepage. As you use your Surface, your homepage will tailor itself to you based on your recent snapshots and apps you used.

If you'd prefer not to have a customized homepage, you can turn the feature off and simply only have a search bar. Here's how to turn this feature off:

- Open **Settings**.
- Select **Privacy & security**.
- Then select **Recall & snapshots**.
- Then select **Advanced settings.**
- Click **Off** under *Recall shows a customized experience using your snapshots*.

To review your previous snapshots, select the timeline from Recall's (preview) navigation bar. Your timeline in Recall (preview) is broken into segments, which are the blocks of time that snapshots of your screen are taken while you were using your Surface Pro for Business, 13-inch (12th Edition). You can hover over your timeline to review your activity in a preview window. Selecting the location on the timeline or selecting the preview window loads the snapshot where you can interact with the content.

When searching with Recall (preview), the results will be listed under the headings of **text matches** and **visual matches**. Matches that are closer to your search are shown first. You'll also notice that some items are listed as one of the following types of matches:

- **Close match**: Close matches typically include at least one of the search terms or images that are representative of a term in your query.
- **Related match**: Matches that share a commonality with the search terms would be considered related.

:::image type="content" border="true" source="../media/recall-search.gif" alt-text="Animation demonstrating search results in Recall (preview) on Surface Pro for Business, 13-inch (12th Edition).":::

You can filter out apps and websites from being saved as snapshots. You can add apps and websites at any time by going to **Settings > Privacy & security > Recall & snapshots** on your Surface.

If you choose to reset Recall (preview), it deletes all your snapshots and resets all your settings for Recall (preview). A reset won't delete snapshots you previously exported to a folder, or snapshots that have been shared previously with an app or website, which have been saved by a third party. To reset Recall (preview):

1. Go to **Settings > Privacy & security > Recall & snapshots > Advanced settings**, then select **Reset Recall**.
2. Confirm that you want to delete all your snapshots and reset your Recall (preview) settings by selecting **Reset**.
3. Authorize reset by using Windows Hello.

## Windows Studio Effects

:::image type="content" border="true" source="../media/windows-studio-effects.gif" alt-text="Animation demonstrating Windows Studio Effects on Surface Pro for Business, 13-inch (12th Edition).":::

Windows Studio Effects utilizes on-device AI on your Surface Pro for Business, 13-inch (12th Edition) to apply special effects to the ultrawide front-facing camera or Dual Studio Mics. These effects include:

- **Background Blur:** A blur effect keeping you in focus. You can select between Standard Blur (a simple gaussian blur effect) and Portrait Blur (a subtle bokeh-style blur effect).
- **Eye Contact:** Subtle correction for a user looking down from the camera to the screen.
- **Auto Framing:** Detects a person in the camera field of view and crops/zooms to keep them centered in frame.
- **Voice Focus:** A microphone effect designed to filter out background noise, ensuring that your voice comes through clearly during video calls.
- **Portrait light:** Improves the quality of lighting on the person in the video in a poorly lit environment, removing strong screen light reflection.
- **Eye contact Teleprompter:** A more aggressive eye contact correction for a user scanning their eyes all around the screen as they read teleprompter content.

To access Windows Studio Effects, do the following:

1. On your desktop, select the **Network**, **Sound**, or **Battery** icon next to the time and date.
2. Select the **Studio Effects** tile to enable and disable the Windows Studio Effects.
3. If the Windows Studio Effects settings need to be reset back to default, select **Reset Effects**.

Windows Studio Effects can also be controlled from the Settings menu:

1. Open **Settings > Bluetooth & devices > Cameras**, then select from the list of **Connected cameras** and open the settings for that camera.
2. You'll see a preview of your front-facing camera display and the **Camera Effects** listed under it.
