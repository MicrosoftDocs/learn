## Microsoft's commitment to data privacy

Microsoft's approach to data privacy is grounded in a time-tested commitment to give you control over the data you put in the cloud—guaranteed through contractual commitments. [The Microsoft Trust Center](https://www.microsoft.com/trust-center/privacy) outlines this commitment across four areas: data control, data location, data security, and data defense.

These principles form Microsoft’s privacy foundation, and they shape the way that products and services are designed.

### You control your data

Your data belongs to you. You can access, modify, or delete it at any time. Microsoft won't use your data without your agreement, and when you provide that agreement, Microsoft uses your data only to provide the services you have chosen.

Your control over your data is reinforced by Microsoft compliance with broadly applicable privacy laws and standards, including ISO/IEC 27018—the world's first international code of practice for cloud privacy.

Microsoft doesn't share your data with advertiser-supported services or mine it for purposes such as marketing research or advertising. When Microsoft deploys subcontractors or subprocessors who require access to your data, those parties can perform only the functions they were engaged to provide. They're bound by the same contractual privacy commitments that Microsoft makes to you, and authorized subprocessors are audited in advance against stringent security and privacy requirements.

### Know where your data is located

When you use Microsoft commercial cloud services, Microsoft helps you choose the service and data location that's right for your business. Through options available in services such as Microsoft Azure, Microsoft Dynamics 365, and Microsoft 365, you can determine where you want your data stored.

Microsoft offers data residency options around the world, helping to ensure that resiliency and compliance requirements can be honored within geographic boundaries. Microsoft backs these capabilities with contractual commitments and transparency about how your data is stored and processed.

### Your data is secured at rest and in transit

Microsoft uses advanced encryption to protect your data both at rest and in transit. Encryption protocols erect barriers against unauthorized access, including two or more independent encryption layers to protect against any single point of compromise.

- **Data at rest**: The Microsoft Cloud employs a wide range of encryption capabilities up to AES-256, giving you the flexibility to choose the solution that best fits your business needs.
- **Data in transit**: Microsoft uses and enables industry-standard encrypted transport protocols, such as Transport Layer Security (TLS) and Internet Protocol Security (IPsec).
- **Encryption keys**: All Microsoft-managed encryption keys are properly secured. Technologies such as Azure Key Vault give you control over passwords, encryption keys, and other secrets.

### Microsoft defends your data

Microsoft defends your data through clearly defined response policies, strong contractual commitments, and, if necessary, the courts. Microsoft's position is that all government requests for your data should be directed to you—not to Microsoft. Microsoft doesn't give any government direct or unfettered access to customer data.

If a government or law enforcement agency requests your data, Microsoft won't disclose it except as you direct or where required by law. Microsoft scrutinizes all government demands to ensure they're legally valid and appropriate. Where legally permitted, Microsoft promptly notifies you and provides a copy of the request, then directs the requesting party to seek the data directly from you.

Microsoft's contractual commitments to enterprise and public sector customers include defending their data. Microsoft will challenge every government request for commercial and public sector customer data where it can lawfully do so, and has a proven track record of successfully challenging government demands in the courts when those demands are inconsistent with the rule of law.

Microsoft is transparent about its specific policies, operational practices, and technologies that protect the privacy of your data across every Microsoft commercial cloud service—and these promises are guaranteed in Microsoft's standard contracts for commercial and public sector customers.
