Protection gaps like exfiltration exposure, overshared sites, and AI interactions reaching sensitive content don't resolve themselves. Each type of gap requires a different action, and applying the wrong remedy wastes effort or creates disruption without reducing risk.

Most environments have several open objectives competing for attention at once. When remediation requires switching between DLP, information protection, and Insider Risk Management consoles independently, gaps stay open longer and policies get scoped to the wrong conditions. There's also no single place to confirm whether a given action actually closed the risk it targeted.

Data Security Posture Management (DSPM) keeps the most common remediation actions inside a single set of objectives. You match each gap to the right pathway, apply the appropriate policy type, and measure whether it worked. The result is shorter time-to-remediation, fewer misaligned policies, and a clear signal when a gap persists despite action.

## Learning objectives

After completing this module, you'll be able to:

- Select remediation approaches based on objective risk indicators
- Apply one-click policies for DLP, sensitivity labels, and Insider Risk Management
- Measure remediation progress through outcome metrics
- Assess and remediate oversharing with data risk assessments

## Prerequisites

- Familiarity with the Microsoft Purview portal and its navigation
- Working knowledge of sensitivity labels and information protection policies
- Working knowledge of data loss prevention (DLP) policies and concepts
- Working knowledge of Insider Risk Management concepts
- Understanding of DSPM's purpose and ability to interpret posture metrics and risk signals
